## 4. Third conditional

**Completa il testo con il 3rd conditional**

> **How I helped someone — and saved my own life!**
> 
> I couldn’t have met Rodney Stuart if he **hadn’t pickpocketed** me on the underground two years ago.  
> Rodney **would have escaped** if I **hadn’t seen** my wallet in his hand as I got off the train. But I saw it, so I followed him home. If I **hadn’t called** the police, they **wouldn’t have arrested** Rodney. I’m sure a lot of people **wouldn’t have done** that if somebody **had stolen** something from them.
> 
> But I rang the doorbell and talked to his mother. She told me about Rodney’s problems with unemployment and drugs, so I decided to give him a chance.
> 
> Anyway, Rodney gave me my wallet and I gave Rodney a job in my shop. If he **hadn’t been** honest with me, I’d **never have offered** Rodney that job. He **certainly wouldn’t have got** that job if he **hadn’t worked** really hard.
> 
> His life **would have been** different, and my life too! You see, if I **hadn’t made** a different decision that day, my life **would have been** very short. Last year a robber came into my shop one night and tried to shoot me. If Rodney Stuart **hadn’t stopped** the robber, I **would have died**.

## 5. 1°, 2° e 3° conditionals

**Riscrivi le frasi usando il condizionale appropriato.**

1. Don’t eat too much, or you’ll be ill.  
    → **If you don’t eat too much, you won’t be ill.**
    
2. Someone saw the burglar so the police caught him.  
    → **If no one had seen the burglar, the police wouldn’t have caught him.**
    
3. I didn’t know about her birthday, so I didn’t buy her a present.  
    → **If I’d known about her birthday, I would have bought her a present.**
    
4. We aren’t rich so we don’t live in a big house.  
    → **If we were rich, we would live in a big house.**
